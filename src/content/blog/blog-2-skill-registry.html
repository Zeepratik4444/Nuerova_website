The Architecture of a Skill

More Than Just Words

A true AI skill consists of four layers: The Trigger (what starts it), The Context (the scoped cluster it queries), The Prompt (the reasoning logic), and The Action (the API call or draft it generates). Together, these form a reliable utility rather than a blind guess.

Why Operations Teams Need Governed Skills

When an operations manager creates a complex workflow to triage customer support tickets or review legal contracts, they shouldn't have to share a 500-word instruction manual. They should simply publish a "Triage Ticket" skill to the registry for the team to use.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can create skills in the registry?

Administrators and designated "Skill Builders" can draft, test, and publish skills to the registry. Once published, any team member with the appropriate RBAC clearance can invoke the skill.

Can a skill interact with our internal databases?

Yes. Nuerova skills can be wired to execute authenticated REST API calls, meaning a skill can fetch live CRM data, update a Jira ticket, or read from a proprietary database during its execution.
