Best Glean Alternatives for Enterprise Teams in 2026

Glean is the dominant name in enterprise AI search, and for good reason — if your primary need is a Google-quality search layer across your company's connected apps, it does that well. But a growing number of enterprise teams are finding that search is only part of what they actually need. They need AI that doesn't just find information but reasons across it, routes it into automated workflows, and scopes what each department or role can access. This post covers the best Glean alternatives for enterprise teams in 2026, what each one is actually good for, and how to choose.

What Makes Teams Look for a Glean Alternative

Glean's core design is search: connect your tools, index the content, let people ask natural-language questions. That's genuinely valuable. The gaps that push teams toward alternatives tend to cluster around three areas.

First, scoping and permissions at the knowledge level. Glean inherits permissions from the source tools — if you can see it in Confluence, you can find it in Glean. That's useful for general search but creates friction for teams that want to scope a specific cluster of knowledge (say, the CS team's account history) to only the people who should reason over it, regardless of what their source-tool permissions technically allow.

Second, automation and workflow execution. Glean answers questions. It doesn't trigger actions, run multi-step agent workflows, or integrate into your operational processes. Teams that want their AI layer to do something — not just say something — need a different architecture.

Third, cost at scale. Glean's per-seat pricing can become significant as adoption spreads beyond knowledge workers into operations, support, and sales teams. Teams with 50–200 seats often start shopping for alternatives around renewal time.

The Key Distinction

Search Finds. Intelligence Acts.

Glean is built around retrieval: surface the right document, thread, or record. The alternatives below are built around reasoning: take the retrieved context and do something useful with it — draft a response, trigger a workflow, generate a structured output. The right choice depends on whether your bottleneck is finding information or acting on it.

The Best Glean Alternatives in 2026

1. Nuerova — Best for Teams That Need Scoped Knowledge + Automation

Nuerova is built around a different primitive than Glean: instead of a single searchable index across all your company's tools, it creates scoped knowledge clusters — isolated vector databases per department, team, or use case — with role-based access control governing what each agent can reason over.

Where it goes further than Glean: Nuerova pairs each knowledge cluster with agent personas and a visual workflow builder. So instead of asking a question and getting a retrieved result, you can wire a trigger (a new Zendesk ticket arrives) to an agent that reasons over the CS team's knowledge cluster, drafts a response, and routes it to a human-in-the-loop approval queue before sending. The knowledge retrieval and the downstream action live in the same platform.

Best for: Operations, CS, and RevOps teams that need both governed knowledge access and automated workflows built on top of it.
Not ideal if: You primarily need broad, org-wide document search without workflow automation.

2. Microsoft Copilot — Best for Microsoft 365-Heavy Teams

If your team lives in Outlook, Teams, Word, and SharePoint, Microsoft Copilot is the path of least resistance. It's deeply embedded in the Microsoft 365 surface, requires no new infrastructure, and is increasingly bundled into enterprise M365 licenses. The trade-off is that it's a personal productivity layer — it helps the individual in front of the screen, not the team as a collective.

Best for: Teams already on M365 whose primary need is individual task acceleration (drafting, summarizing, meeting notes).
Not ideal if: You need cross-tool reasoning, scoped team knowledge, or workflow automation beyond Microsoft's ecosystem.

3. Guru — Best for Customer-Facing Knowledge Bases

Guru sits in the knowledge-management space rather than the AI-search space. It's built around verified, curated cards — bite-sized pieces of knowledge that teams (especially support and sales) can reference quickly. It has AI features for surfacing relevant cards during conversations, but it's fundamentally a structured knowledge base, not a reasoning layer over unstructured data.

Best for: Support and sales teams that need a curated, maintained knowledge base for customer-facing conversations.
Not ideal if: Your knowledge is unstructured (Slack threads, Jira tickets, Confluence pages) and you need AI to reason over it rather than surface manually curated cards.

4. Notion AI — Best for Teams Already in Notion

Notion AI is deeply embedded in Notion's document and database layer. If your team's knowledge already lives in Notion, the AI features (Q&A over your workspace, page generation, summaries) add meaningful value with zero migration cost. The limitation is the same as Copilot's: it's scoped to what lives in Notion, and it operates at the document level rather than reasoning across multi-tool operational data.

Best for: Teams whose knowledge is primarily in Notion and who want AI assistance on that content specifically.
Not ideal if: Your data is spread across Slack, Jira, Salesforce, and Zendesk — Notion AI won't touch it.

Who Should Pick Glean Anyway

Glean is the right choice if your primary need is broad, org-wide search across a large content corpus — thousands of Confluence pages, a full Google Drive, a deep Salesforce record history — and you don't need the AI layer to trigger actions or enforce team-level knowledge scoping. Large enterprises with mature document libraries and a clear "help me find things faster" use case are Glean's home turf.

If your need is more operational — you want the AI to reason over a specific team's context and do something with the result, not just surface a document — the alternatives above are better fits.

The Bottom Line

The best Glean alternative for your team depends on what Glean isn't doing for you. If it's workflow automation and scoped team knowledge, Nuerova is the closest architectural match. If it's Microsoft 365 depth, Copilot wins. If it's curated customer-facing knowledge, Guru is designed for that. And if your knowledge already lives in Notion, Notion AI is the zero-migration option.

The common thread: every meaningful alternative is built around a different primary job. Match the tool to the job, not the marketing.
