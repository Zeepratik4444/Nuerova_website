The Concept of the Knowledge Cluster

A Knowledge Cluster is a dynamic, living repository of a team's operating context. Unlike a static wiki, a cluster connects directly to the digital exhaust of where work actually happens: Slack channels, Jira boards, Zendesk tickets, and active Confluence pages.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a cluster forget outdated information?

Yes. Nuerova utilizes recency weighting and allows administrators to deprecate specific sources, files, or timeframe indexes to ensure the agent doesn't act on stale policies.

Do we need engineering resources to set this up?

No. Knowledge clusters are configured via a visual dashboard. You simply authenticate with your SaaS applications via OAuth, select the channels or folders to ingest, and Nuerova handles the embeddings, chunking, and vectorization.
